Academic Achievement Awards
Department of Chemistry & Biochemistry

These awards are given annually at the end of spring semester to celebrate the accomplishments of students within our department.
Students may not apply for these awards as they are determined by Department faculty nomination only.


Undergraduate Awards:

  • ACS Analytical Chemistry Award: Given to the student who exhibits the greatest expertise in analytical chemistry.
  • ACS Polymer Chemistry Award: Given to a student who has completed 320A during the previous Fall or will complete the 320B course in the Spring semester.
  • ACS Undergraduate Award in Organic Chemistry: Given to the student who exhibits the greatest expertise in organic chemistry.
  • American Institute of Chemists (AIC) Baccalaureate Award Chemistry: Given to a graduating senior who is deemed to have an outstanding record of service and academic accomplishment in chemistry.
  • American Institute of Chemists (AIC) Baccalaureate Award Biochemistry: Given to a graduating senior who is deemed to have an outstanding record of service and academic accomplishment in biochemistry.
  • Analytical Chemistry: Given to the outstanding student of analytical chemistry who has completed both Chem 251 and Chem 451.
  • Biochemistry Award: Given to the outstanding student of biochemistry who has completed Chem 441A and has or will complete 441B in the current academic year.
  • Freshman Chemistry Award: Given to the outstanding student of first semester general chemistry, Chem 111A.
  • Spyros Pathos IV: Given to the outstanding student of second semester general chemistry, Chem 111B.
  • Horalek Award: Given to the graduating student who has rendered an outstanding service to the department.
  • Hypercube Award: Given to a student who has demonstrated excellence in computer applications in chemistry.
  • Inorganic Chemistry: Given to the outstanding student of inorganic chemistry who has completed Chem 431.
  • John H. Stern Award in Physical Chemistry: Given to the outstanding physical chemistry student who has completed Chem 371A and is in the process of completing, or has completed, Chem 371B.
  • Organic Chemistry Award: Given to the outstanding student of organic chemistry who has completed Chem 320A and completed Chem 320B in the Fall Semester of the current academic year.
  • Scoggins Award: Given to an outstanding graduating student who plans to continue graduate work in a health related profession such as medicine, dentistry, pharmacy, or optometry.
  • Departmental Honors for BS candidates: This award is based on service and scholarship and may be given up to three students at the undergraduate levels. The honors will be noted on the transcripts and diplomas of the students.
  • Outstanding Graduate in CNSM Award: The award is sponsored by the University Alumni Association and given by the College of Natural Sciences and Mathematics to the most outstanding graduating undergraduate.
  • Rhodes Award: Given by the College of Natural Sciences and Mathematics to an outstanding departmental graduating senior.

Graduate Awards:
  • American Institute of Chemists (AIC) Graduate Award Chemistry: Given to an outstanding graduate student in chemistry.
  • American Institute of Chemists (AIC) Graduate Award Biochemistry: Given to an outstanding graduate student in biochemistry.
  • Outstanding Teaching Associate Award: Given to an outstanding teaching associate in chemistry or biochemistry.
  • Departmental Honors for MS candidates: This award is based on service and scholarship and may be given up to three students at the graduate levels. The honors will be noted on the transcripts and diplomas of the students.
  • Graduate Dean's List of Scholars and Artists: Given by the College of Natural Sciences and Mathematics to three students. This is an honor accorded to only the top 1% of the master's students.
  • Outstanding MS Thesis Award: Given to the student whose Master's thesis is judged to be the most outstanding by our Department.
  • Outstanding Graduate in CNSM Award: The award is sponsored by the University Alumni Association and given by the College of Natural Sciences and Mathematics to the most outstanding graduating graduate.

Competitive Awards
Department of Chemistry & Biochemistry

These awards are given annually: to be considered, eligible students must submit completed applications via the Beach Scholarship webpage HERE

  • Robert B. Henderson Award: Given to a graduating BS or MS student who plans to enter a Ph.D. program in chemistry or biochemistry and who best demonstrates the example of scholarship of the late Dr. Robert B. Henderson.
  • Kenneth L. Marsi Scholarship: Given to an outstanding junior or senior majoring in chemistry or biochemistry.
  • Michael Monahan Memorial Summer Research Scholarship: Given to an outstanding undergraduate or master's student to support summer research.
  • Wynston Award in Biochemistry: Given to an outstanding junior or master's student in biochemistry planning to enter a health related professional school upon graduation.
  • McAbee-Overstreet Graduate Research Scholarship: Given to an outstanding graduate student in biochemistry for excellence in scholarship and commitment to research.

College of Natural Sciences & Mathematics, University, and CSU Level
Competitive Awards & Student Fellowship Programs

These programs at the College and University levels are open for students to apply to each academic year. Students should consult the appropriate CSULB links below for more information.

  • McNair Scholars Program: Designed to increase the number of students from underrepresented segments of society and disadvantaged backgrounds who have demonstrated strong, academic potential to go on to graduate study.
  • Maximizing Access to Research Careers (MARC-U*STAR) Program: Given to students who are interested in the biomedical research field. Will receive financial support to carry out undergraduate research with a CSULB faculty member.
  • Research Initiative for Scientific Enhancement (RISE) Program: Given to students who are interested in the biomedical research field. Will receive financial support to carry out undergraduate research with a CSULB faculty member.
  • CSU Louis Stokes Alliances for minority Participation (LSAMP) Research Fellows Program: Given to students who have faced or face social, educational or economic barriers, majoring in science, technology, mathematics and engineering (STEM).
  • Bridges to the Baccalaureate Program: Designed to strengthen the relationship that exists between two key community colleges, Long Beach City College and Cerritos Community College, and CSULB. Selected students and faculty spend 9 weeks in the summer in CSULB laboratories participating in hands-on research.
  • Keck Energy Materials Research Program (KEMP): Provides an exciting, cutting-edge, and research-driven educational experience for undergraduate students in the high impact science of energy related materials.
  • Robert Noyce Scholarship Program: Award forgivable loans of $15,000 per year to qualified STEM majors who plan on earning a secondary school teaching credential in mathematics or science.
  • Women and Philanthropy Undergraduate Research and Creative Activity Scholarship: Students who will participate in research, scholarly and creative activity under the direct supervision of a faculty mentor.
  • SLD Student Academic Travel Grant: Support students wishing to enhance their educational experience by attending academic conferences, workshops, seminars and/or lectures.
  • ASI Students Travel Fund: Support student participation in academic or professional conferences.
  • University Scholarship Search Form: Filter possible awards based on academic year, major, and other criteria.
  • CSUPERB: The CSU Program for Research in Biotechnology offers several awards and grants to students and faculty in the CSU.
